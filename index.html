Shot list — what to send, in priority order

The duplicates are almost all a supply problem: sections cycle through a family's photo pool, and when the pool is smaller than the number of slots, images repeat. These batches would clear the bulk of the table below. The die-casting batch has been delivered and applied — the five below remain.

  • SHEET METAL
    5–6 fabricated part photos (brackets, enclosures-style weldments, formed panels — finished parts, not texture close-ups). The hub page has 10+ capability cards drawing on only 4 photos, so tread plate and perforated panel each show 3–4 times on one page.
  • CNC
    4–6 machined part photos (milled housings, turned shafts, finished billet parts on neutral background). The CNC hub cycles 6 photos across 10 route cards plus the gallery — every image appears twice or more. Current shots are also phone-quality.
  • INVESTMENT CASTING
    4–5 real investment castings (stainless / carbon steel parts, shell or finished). The family borrows one die-casting photo (valve-bodies.jpg) that repeats on all six investment casting pages, in both the gallery and the industries strip.
  • FINISHING
    3–4 photos each for powder coating and anodizing/metal finishing. Both pages fill their entire four-tile gallery with a single photo repeated four times.
  • US FACILITY
    3–4 photos of the Seattle operation (floor, machines, parts). The USA manufacturing page repeats one generic machine-shop photo across all four gallery tiles.
  • ARTICLE THUMBS
    A wider set of editorial/detail shots for article cards (metal textures, measurement, stock, tooling — 10–15 varied images). The blog index reuses one stock photo on 15 cards and another on 9. Anything on-theme works; variety is the goal.
Already requested India facility photos (hero, floor, inspection, parts) and sand-casting part photography are in the earlier Slack request — same batch, still needed. Sand casting doesn't appear in this duplicate list only because its low-res repeats were swapped for stand-ins today.
